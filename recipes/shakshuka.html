Shakshuka

A delicious spicy egg dish for those chilly winter days

My preferred egg dish is Shakshuka, it's the perfect comfort food particularly during cold days because it's spicy and satisfying.
This meal has its roots in North Africa, particularly in Tunisia, but it has gained popularity as a Middle Eastern cuisine.

Although there are several shakshuka variations, the classic recipe entails poaching eggs in a blend of tomatoes, green peppers, onions, garlic, and spices.

Ingredients

  • Two tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil
  • One onion, chopped
  • One green pepper, chopped
  • One garlic clove, minced
  • One teaspoon of sea salt
  • one teaspoon of ground coriander
  • one teaspoon of paprika
  • half a teaspoon of ground cumin
  • half a teaspoon of pepper
  • a pinch of red chili flakes
  • four ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • half a cup of tomato sauce (or passata)
  • one heaped cup of fresh spinach
  • three to four large eggs
  • one quarter of a cup of fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large cast iron pan on medium heat. Add the onions, green peppers, spinach, garlic, and the spices.
  2. Add the chopped tomatoes and the tomato sauce. Bring it to a simmer, until the tomato mixture begins to reduce.
  3. Using a wooden spoon, make three to four indentations in the tomato mixture and gently crack an egg into each indention.
  4. Reduce the heat, cover the pan, and cook on low until the egg whites are set.
  5. Uncover and add the fresh parsley. Serve with warm pita or your choice of crusty bread.
